HEALTH CARE & FITNESS

WASH HANDS

It is very very important to wash your hands every 30 minutes with soap and water or an alcohol based sanitizer.

GARGLE

Gargle regularly with lukewarm water with turmeric and salt: Boil ¼ glass of water with turmeric and salt and then top up the glass with water.

VITAMIN C

If you cannot get enough natural sources, take a vitamin C tablet regularly Vitamins not only help in repairing worn out tissues and building immunity.

SPECIAL TEAS

Make a concoction with turmeric milk/giloy water/ black pepper-ginger and tulsi tea and have it every morning. This is actually good for the entire family.

EXERCISE

Practice proper exercise or pranayama – specifically breathing exercises like – Bharastika, Anulom Vilom Pranayama, savasana, Kapalbhati Pranayama, Nadi Shodhan Pranayama.

BALANCED DIET

Have proper balanced meals at regular intervals, and try adding garlic-ginger-chilli paste to the meal. Vitamins not only help in repairing worn out tissues and building immunity.

MAGNESIUM INTAKE

Step up Magnesium intake: this helps in keeping your heart healthy, maintaining your immune system and bone health. Mainly found in whole grains, nuts, fresh fruit, and vegetables.

PROTEIN INTAKE

Traditional Indian sattu (jaw or chana powder) buttermilk/water is a great source of protein, and also a probiotic.

FRUITS AND VEGETABLES

Increase the intake of citrus fruits or vegetables with high vitamin C content: oranges/ amla/ strawberries / sweet lime/ spinach.

STAY HYDRATED

Stay Hydrated Drink up to 8-10 glasses of water every day, to stay hydrated. Hydration will help flush out the toxins from the body and lower the chances of flu. Other alternatives include juices made of citrus fruits and coconut water, to beat the heat.

CONSUME SOFT EATABLES

To aid chewing and digestion, you could consider thick soups, pureed vegetables, powdered seedsand nuts, thick dal vegetable khichdi, scrambled eggs, soft fruits, stewed fruits or custard, bone broth and vegetable stews etc.

DONT COMPROMISE ON SLEEP

Good snooze time for 7-8 hours is the best way to help your body build immunity; lesser sleep will leave you tired and impair your brain activity. The lack of sleep will prevent the body from resting and this will impair other bodily functions that will have a direct impact on your immunity.
